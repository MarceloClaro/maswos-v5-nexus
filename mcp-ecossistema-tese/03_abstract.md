# ABSTRACT

This thesis investigates the Model Context Protocols (MCP) ecosystem, a standardized infrastructure for integrating generative artificial intelligence systems with external data sources, tools, and educational systems. The study analyzes the underlying Transformer architecture of the MCP ecosystem, its validation mechanisms, technical limitations, and the potential impact on Brazilian education and scientific production, with a specific focus on the Northeast region and the Backlands of Ceará, particularly the municipality of Crateús. The research is structured in six chapters ranging from the theoretical foundations of context protocols to the practical implications for democratizing access to artificial intelligence in less favored educational contexts. The results demonstrate that the MCP ecosystem represents a significant evolution in AI systems interoperability, with direct implications for reproducible academic research and for reducing inequalities in access to advanced educational technologies. The methodology employed combines systematic literature review, technical architecture evaluation, and prospective impact analysis. The conclusions indicate that, despite implementation challenges and security limitations not yet fully resolved, the MCP ecosystem has transformative potential for scientific education in Brazil, especially in regions characterized by limited infrastructure such as the Northeastern Backlands.

**Keywords:** Model Context Protocol, Artificial Intelligence, Education, Scientific Production, Northeast Region, Ceará Backlands, Transformer Architecture.
